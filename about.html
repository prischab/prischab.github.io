About me

I'm a third-year B.Tech student specializing in Artificial Intelligence & Data Science at MIT World Peace University, Pune.

I was brought up in Muscat, Oman, where I spent my entire school life before moving to Pune for my bachelor's degree. That mix of growing up abroad and studying in India has shaped how I approach problems — with curiosity, adaptability, and an eye for how technology serves real people.

My work spans the full lifecycle of machine learning — from training models and engineering data pipelines to deploying them as usable applications. I've built everything from an embedding-based facial recognition system running on a Raspberry Pi to a FastAPI service that predicts chest X-ray diagnoses.

With two published research papers and a genuine love for technical writing, I care as much about communicating ideas clearly as I do about building them. I'm proficient across Python, PyTorch, Keras, SQL and scikit-learn — and always looking for the next problem worth solving.
